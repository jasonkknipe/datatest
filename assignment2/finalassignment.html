Shootings in NYC (2006 - 2019)



Over recent decades, gun control in the US has been one of the most widely discussed news topics worldwide. Many differing opinions with constitution, faith and protection have driven a wedge between portions of the American public. This analysis aims to break down the use of such weapons in the largest city in the US, New York City.

Police shooting records over a 14-year period from 2006-2019 have been collected and classified based on a range of factors including the details of time, location, the victim, and the perpetrator where possible. These have been plotted over a geographic map of NYC to ultimately produce the following visualisations.

There are 21,626 total incidents, each with its own story to tell.

The 75th
Police Precinct,
Brooklyn

Shootings are rampant throughout New York City, especially in the borough of Brooklyn. Precinct 75 is well known to be the most troubled police precinct in the city, some of which is contributed to by the high concentration of public housing in the area.
Throughout much of the US, the political movement to ‘defund the police’ has gained massive traction. Brooklyn Public Safety Committee Chairman Vincent Riggins agrees, stating that “regardless of how much they spend on the police, they have to come up with money to address housing, jobs, entrepreneurship initiatives, schools where kids can learn a trade with their hands to become gainfully employed in their own businesses."



Despite the somewhat shocking figures, it may surprise some that the vast majority of these incidents are non-lethal, and many aren’t acts of aggression, more so self-defence.

Only 19% of incidents were statistically considered to be incidents of murder.



The unfortunate reality of these incidents is that they are not at all racially neutral. Over the observed period, over 70% of the affected victims were black.

City Journal reports that in addition to this, around 74% of suspects are also black

The NYC
SAFE Act, 2013


Following the Sandy Hook Elementary School shooting in December of 2012, gun laws around the US were tightened – including in Ney York State. On January 15, 2013, legislation was passed which introduced many new laws and restrictions around the purchase and ownership of firearms. These changes included making many categories of advanced firearms illegal, as well as requiring much more thorough background checks for individuals wishing to purchase ammunition. Following these changes, reported firearm incidents in have NYC decreased by around 38%.
